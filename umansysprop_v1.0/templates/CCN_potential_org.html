Calculate the CCN activation potential a mixed organic aerosol particle. Users need to define the composition accordign to relative molar concentrations. For example, an equimolar Succinic acid - Oxalic acid particle might be defined as C(CC(=O)O)C(=O)O 1.0 and C(=O)(C(=O)O)O 1.0.

Results are obtained from finding the critical point, or maximum point, on the relationship between saturation ratio of water vapour and droplet size. It has been common place to use Kohler theory for this purpose, for which more information can be found in numerous sources such as Topping and McFiggans 2010.

  • Topping, D. O. and McFiggans, G.: Tight coupling of particle size, number and composition in atmospheric cloud droplet activation, Atmos. Chem. Phys., 12, 3253-3260, doi:10.5194/acp-12-3253-2012, 2012.

    It is important to note that only water is allowed to re-quilibrate between the gas and condensed phase in traditional Kohler theory and in these calculations. Similarly, in the present simulations users have to define the surface tension value at the point of activation.
