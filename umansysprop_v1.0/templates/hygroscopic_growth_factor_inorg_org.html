Calculate the hygroscopic growth factor of a mixed organic-inorganic aerosol particle in sub-saturated humid conditions. Users need to define the inorganic composition according to relative molar concentrations. For example, an equimolar Ammonium Sulphate - Succinic acid particle might have molar concentrations of the [NH4+] ion of 2.0 , [O-]S([O-])(=O)=O as 1.0. and C(CC(=O)O)C(=O)O as 1.0.

Using the form below you can either upload a text file constaining a list of SMILEs strings or enter each SMILES string individually. To add a new compound simply click on the 'Add' button.

Inorganic ions covered in our simulations, with the associated SMILES string, are:

  • [Na+] Sodium cation
  • [NH4+] Ammonium cation
  • [K+] Potassium cation
  • [Ca+2] Calcium cation
  • [Mg+2] Magnesium cation
  • [Cl-] Chloride anion
  • [O-][N+]([O-])=O Nitrate anion
  • [O-]S([O-])(=O)=O Sulphate anion

Results are obtained from finding the equilibrium water content at a user defined RH and comparing the droplet size to a specific dry size. As with CCN activation predictions, more information can be found in numerous sources such as Topping and McFiggans 2010.

  • Topping, D. O. and McFiggans, G.: Tight coupling of particle size, number and composition in atmospheric cloud droplet activation, Atmos. Chem. Phys., 12, 3253-3260, doi:10.5194/acp-12-3253-2012, 2012.
